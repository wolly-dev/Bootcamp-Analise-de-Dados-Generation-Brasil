# 📊 Performance and User Feedback Analysis – NetGuard Pro

> **Data Analysis Exercise with AI Support**  
> Data Analysis Bootcamp | June 30 2026

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This repository documents a **practical Data Analysis exercise** conducted during a bootcamp, with a dual objective:

1. **Simulate a real-world software evaluation scenario** (the fictional **NetGuard Pro**), using quantitative data (server metrics) and qualitative data (user feedback).
2. **Practice the use of generative AI as a support tool for data analysts**, throughout all stages of the process: from interpreting and categorizing large volumes of textual data to structuring technical reports and strategic recommendations.

The exercise was deliberately designed so that the analyst acted as **curator and strategist**, while the AI acted as an **intelligent assistant** — streamlining repetitive tasks, suggesting patterns, and organizing information, without replacing human critical judgment.

**Key practical learnings from the exercise:**
- How to integrate AI into a data analyst's workflow.
- How to extract actionable insights from unstructured data (texts).
- How to cross-reference qualitative and quantitative data to support decisions.
- How to structure a clear, objective technical report with prioritized recommendations.

---

## 🎯 Analysis Objectives

- Evaluate user experience quality based on feedback and performance metrics.
- Identify the main bugs or reported issues.
- Propose changes and improvements based on the analyzed data.
- Structure a clear and objective technical report for decision-making.

---

## 🤖 The Role of AI in This Exercise

Unlike a traditional data analysis, this exercise was planned to **actively integrate generative AI tools** into the analytical process. AI was used as:

| Stage | How AI was used |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Reading and interpretation** | Assisted in reading and interpreting large volumes of textual data (50 user feedbacks). |
| **Categorization** | Suggested functionality categories and semantic groupings for the comments. |
| **Sentiment analysis** | Supported the identification of tones (positive, neutral, negative) in each feedback. |
| **Report structuring** | Organized the information according to the technical template format, ensuring coherence and flow. |
| **Recommendation generation** | Proposed solutions based on identified patterns, which were validated and prioritized by the analyst. |

**Important:** at no point did AI replace the analyst's judgment. All conclusions, prioritizations, and final recommendations were **manually validated and refined**, ensuring that the final product reflected the strategic vision and critical sense of the professional.

> 🧠 **Pedagogical objective:** This exercise demonstrates that AI is an **augmentation tool**, not a replacement. The analyst maintains control of the process, while AI accelerates operational tasks and expands information processing capacity.

## 📁 Data Structure

### 1. Performance Report (`SA-AIR_Performance_Report_pt-BR.xlsx`)
- Contains metrics from **100 servers**, including:
  - CPU, memory, and disk usage.
  - Latency, packet loss, and connection success rate.
  - Failover events, error types, and affected services.

### 2. User Feedback (`SA-AIR_User_Feedback_pt-BR.xlsx`)
- Contains **50 user reviews** (fictional), including:
  - Star ratings (1 to 5).
  - Qualitative comments on functionalities.
  - Specific suggestions and complaints.

### 3. Technical Report Template (`SA-AIR_Template-TECH_pt-BR.docx`)
- Structure used to organize the analysis findings.
- Includes functionality categories, problem identification, and executive summary.

## 🧠 Applied Methodology

The analysis was conducted in **5 main milestones**, with **continuous AI integration** at every stage:

| Milestone | Description | AI Participation |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **1. Data Preparation** | Review and organization of datasets (spreadsheets). | Assistance in consistency validation and identification of relevant fields. |
| **2. User Feedback Analysis** | Categorization by functionality, sentiment evaluation, and suggestion extraction. | **Central role:** semantic categorization of 50 texts, suggestion of groupings, and identification of sentiment patterns. |
| **3. Performance Data Analysis** | Pattern identification, statistical calculations, and cross-referencing with feedback. | Automatic generation of descriptive statistics and initial correlations. |
| **4. Findings Summary** | Creation of a summary document with insights and recommendations. | Structuring of the report text, maintaining logical coherence between sections. |
| **5. Review and Refinement** | Final adjustments, reference validation, and delivery preparation. | Consistency checking of references and suggestions for writing improvements. |

> 💡 **Exercise differentiator:** AI was not used as an "automatic answer generator," but as an **intelligent copilot** — suggesting, organizing, and accelerating, while the analyst validated, adjusted, and provided the final strategic tone.

## 🔍 Key Findings

### ✅ Strengths
- **Failover** and **network stability** (low packet loss) are highly praised.
- **Session and disk monitoring** is considered complete and reliable.

### ⚠️ Identified Issues
| Issue | Frequency | Severity |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| High CPU usage and dashboard lag | 36% of feedback | **Critical** |
| Firewall configuration complexity | 24% of feedback | **High** |
| Lack of intelligent bandwidth prioritization | 18% of feedback | **Medium** |

---

## 🛠️ Proposed Recommendations

1. **Optimize CPU usage** – Review architecture, implement caching, and separate critical processes.
2. **Simplify firewall configuration** – Create setup wizard and visual templates.
3. **Implement QoS for bandwidth** – Dynamically prioritize critical tasks.

## 💭 Reflection on the Use of AI in Data Analysis

This exercise demonstrated that generative AI can be a **powerful ally** for data analysts, especially in tasks involving:

- **Unstructured textual data:** where AI accelerates categorization and sentiment analysis.
- **Report generation:** where AI assists in structuring and formatting, allowing the analyst to focus on strategic content.
- **Pattern suggestion:** where AI identifies initial correlations that can be validated or refuted by the analyst.

**Lessons learned:**
- AI is efficient for **low-cognitive-effort, high-volume** tasks, such as reading and categorizing texts.
- **Human judgment** remains essential for prioritization, context validation, and decision-making.
- The **analyst's curation** of AI outputs is what ensures the final quality of the work.

> "AI does not replace the analyst; it amplifies their ability to process information and focus on what truly matters: interpreting, prioritizing, and recommending."
